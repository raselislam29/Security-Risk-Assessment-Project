# Executive Summary

## Objective

Perform an organization-wide cybersecurity risk assessment to identify material risks across infrastructure, applications, and data, then prioritize remediation actions aligned with NIST CSF and SOC 2 requirements.

## Assessment Coverage

- Infrastructure controls: IAM, endpoint protection, network segmentation, vulnerability and patch management, backup/recovery.
- Application controls: secure SDLC, code and dependency risk, secrets handling, application logging and monitoring.
- Data controls: classification, access governance, encryption, retention, and resilience.

## Key Findings

- High-risk themes included privileged access governance, endpoint patching coverage, network segmentation, secrets management, and backup restoration validation.
- Multiple medium-risk gaps were identified in centralized logging, SDLC control consistency, and vendor reassessment cadence.
- Existing controls were present in most domains but lacked maturity, automation, or measurable assurance.

## Risk Profile Snapshot

- Total risks identified: 10
- High priority risks: 6
- Medium priority risks: 4
- Low priority risks: 0

## Business Impact

- Reduced probability of high-severity incidents through prioritized remediation planning.
- Improved accountability by assigning risk owners and target dates.
- Enhanced audit and compliance readiness by mapping gaps to NIST CSF and SOC 2 criteria.

## Recommended Next 90 Days

1. Implement privileged access management and formal access recertification.
2. Enforce enterprise patch compliance and endpoint inventory completeness.
3. Centralize security logging with actionable detections for high-risk events.
4. Institutionalize secure SDLC gates and secrets management controls.
5. Launch recurring recovery tests and third-party reassessment cycles.
